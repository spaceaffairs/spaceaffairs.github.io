The International Agency for Space Affairs (IASA) claims space for life. It recruits experts in space exploration to support the development and dissemination of knowledge, cultural practices and technologies that benefit life on Earth. IASA also supports educational projects with an emphasis on increasing diversity in modes of inhabiting the planet.

Impact of O

The impact of O did not go unnoticed:

"On 29 June this year, Earth racked up an unusual record: its shortest day since the 1960s, when scientists began measuring the planet’s rotation with high-precision atomic clocks. Broadly speaking, Earth completes one full turn on its axis every 24 hours. That single spin marks out a day and drives the cycle of sunrise and sunset that has shaped patterns of life for billions of years. But the curtains fell early on 29 June, with midnight arriving 1.59 milliseconds sooner than expected." (The Guardian, 01.08.2022)

Affiliated Artists

Norbert Pape

Norbert Pape is a dancer, choreographer, mathematician and pedagogue based in Berlin. He collaborated and toured internationally with artists such as Mette Ingvartsen, Antje Pfundtner, Christine Cuipke, Noé Soulier, Richard Siegal, William Forsythe, Nancy Stark Smith, Jan Ritsema, Bojana Cvejic, Dieter Heitkamp. His work has been presented in museums and venues such as Hamburger Bahnhof Berlin (2021), Mousonturm Frankfurt, Frankfurt LAB, Schwankhalle Bremen, PACT Zollverein, Festspielhaus Sankt Poelten, Theater Altenburg-Gera, Elizabeth-Kirche Berlin.

He studied classical and contemporary dance at the Hochschule für Musik und Darstellende Kunst Frankfurt am Main, obtained an M.A. in Choreography and Performance at the Institute for Applied Theater Sciences in Gießen under the direction of Prof. Bojana Kunst. Further, he recently obtained a B.Sc. (FU Berlin) and M.Sc. (KU Leuven) in Mathematics with research focus on Category Theory and Type Theory.

As a co-founder of ID Frankfurt and active member of the Performing Arts Forum, he has been committed to matters of self-organisation and creation of infrastructure that makes space for experimental and critical modes of production, exchange and research.

Fall 2021, he co-founded the International Agency for Space Affairs (IASA) together with Pêdra Costa, Göksu Kunak, Yuri Shimaoka and Elena Polzer.

Göksu Kunak

Göksu Kunak (Ankara, 1985) a.k.a Gucci Chunk is a writer, performer and performance maker based in Berlin. Göksu’s interest lies in queer methodologies (especially chronopolitics) and hybrid texts that deal with the performative lingo(s) of contemporary lifestyles. Imagining a situation out of real encounters, thinking about Arabesk culture and how this can enter into the words of a Westernized Eastern are some of the artist’s concerns.

Pêdra Costa

Pêdra Costa is a ground breaking, formative Brazilian, urban anthropologist and performer based in Berlin that utilizes intimacy to connect with collectivity. They work with their body to create fragmented epistemologies of queer communities within ongoing colonial legacies. Their work aims to decode violence and transform failure whilst tapping into the powers of resilient knowledge from a plethora of subversive ancestralities that have been integral anti-colonial and necropolitical survival.

Yuri Shimaoka

Yuri Shimaoka is a Berlin based dancer and martial artist from Nagano, Japan. Her expertise lies in bridging inner and outer space via techniques drawn from ballet, physics, Budo (Japanese martial arts) and music.

Elena Polzer

Elena Polzer interessiert sich intensiv für Themen wie Kollektivität, Feminismus, gewaltfreie Kommunikation, die Bedeutung des Handwerks, Textilien, Ritual und Magie. Sie arbeitet seit 2001 als selbstständige Produzentin, Übersetzerin (dt-eng-jap), Dramaturgin und Performerin mit einer großen Bandbreite von Tanzschaffenden zusammen. Am besten bekannt ist sie als eine der Gründerinnen von ehrliche arbeit – freies Kulturbüro, einer Produktionsplattform für freie darstellende Künste. 2021 gründeten sie zusammen die Warp & Weft GbR für das gleichnamige, gemeinsam konzipierte Projekt welches sich mit den anhaltenden Nachwirkungen der deutschen Kolonialgeschichte beschäftigt.
